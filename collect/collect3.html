Recovery?

I'm in the process of filtering out my collection for the severalth time, hoping to decrease my inventory by at least a third. I've settled into a finite number of groups of figures, although each group could potentially be very large. They are, in no particular order: 6" comic characters (DC, Marvel, Spawn, other), 8" movie figures (Universal Monsters, Movie Maniacs, others), dragons, and a few specialty pieces. That's still a lot, but it is progress. I mean, I could be collecting animated, Famous Covers, Megos, Super Powers, GI Joes, 5", Transformers, Dragonball, and many more. I am also making great strides in organizing and cleaning up the figures I am keeping. In addition, I'm finally taking most of those expensive DC Direct figures off of their cards.

Conclusion

Now you be the judge - avid enthusiast or geek with obsessive/compulsive disorder? Only time will tell. If nothing else, it keeps me occupied and busy, and gives me something to strive for. In a few years I may even get out of the hobby altogether. Who knows how many figures are they going to release over the next five years? Ten years? I would have no place to put them all. Maybe I could bury them in mason jars in the backyard...
